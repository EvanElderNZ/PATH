🌊 PATH: A Coastal Erosion Risk Tool Arctic Communities

Build a community asset inventory, have a consultant map the shoreline recession along a series of transects, and then turn the info into clear vulnerability and risk maps that a community can use to address their erosion risk.

How this tool works

Two people, two sheets. New here? Download the walkthrough and keep it beside you.

  1. Community Administrator — lists community assets (name, location, consequence) on the Asset Inventory Generator page, optionally sets the time horizons, and downloads the asset inventory to share.
  2. Consultant / engineer — fills a separate transect sheet: for each shoreline transect, its coordinates, its orientation (the compass bearing the shoreline recedes toward) and the projected recession (m) for a Baseline and a High scenario at three time horizons. The transects span far enough along the shore to cover every asset.
  3. Community Administrator — uploads both sheets on the Erosion Hazard Input page; the tool maps the recession envelopes, tests which assets fall inside them, and produces vulnerability & risk maps plus a multi-page PDF.

Community & assets Community Administrator

List each community asset: name, latitude, longitude (decimal degrees) and consequence if it were lost. That is all the administrator provides. Confirm names and spellings with the community.

Time horizons optional

You may set the three assessment years for the consultant. Leave blank to let the consultant choose.

Map settings

The shoreline and the map orientation come straight from the transect coordinates and bearings — nothing to set there. Adjust the low-vulnerability buffer if needed. Confirm against local knowledge.

Download the results

Builds and downloads a poster-size (A0) PDF package directly — a title page, every map and grid on its own page, plain-English explainer pages, and reference tables at the end. No print dialog: the file saves straight to your downloads.

About. Maps are drawn shore-aligned (sea at the bottom; the north arrow rotates to match) from the transect shoreline coordinates, with the receded shoreline placed by moving each transect point inland along its bearing by the projected recession. Lines are smoothed between transects and extended to the map edges — schematic, not surveyed. Vertical scale may be exaggerated (labelled on the map). Read alongside Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit and local knowledge.

Reference: Inuit Circumpolar Council, Circumpolar Inuit Protocols for Equitable and Ethical Engagement (EEE). Independent; not endorsed by the ICC.
